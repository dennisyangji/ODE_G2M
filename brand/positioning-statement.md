## Key Differentiators (Ranked by Impact)

### 1. AI-Native, Not AI-Retrofitted
ODE was architected from day one with AI at its core. This isn't a chatbot bolted onto a 1990s codebase. The AI understands engineering intent — it can generate Modelica models from natural language requirements, run simulations, analyze results, and suggest design improvements. Competitors are adding AI features to fundamentally legacy architectures; ODE's AI is structural.

### 2. Unified Engineering Platform
One platform for CAD (Geometry), CAE (Component + Vision), MBSE (System), documentation (Paper), and real-time visualization (Interaction). Engineers stop losing time switching between tools, exporting/importing files, and maintaining data consistency. Everything lives in one environment.

### 3. Modelica + LLM Synergy
This is ODE's technical moat. Modelica is equation-based and structured — making it inherently machine-friendly. While competitors struggle to make AI work with proprietary binary formats, ODE's Modelica foundation gives AI a native language for physics. The result: AI that can reason about physical systems, not just pattern-match.

### 4. Cloud-Native, Zero-Install
Open a browser and start engineering. No downloads, no IT approval cycles, no version conflicts. Real-time collaboration means teams work together simultaneously — not sending files back and forth. This removes the biggest adoption barrier for new users and teams.

### 5. Open Standards First
Built on Modelica 4.0, FMI 2.0/3.0, and SysML v1.6. This means no vendor lock-in, interoperability with existing tools and models, and alignment with international engineering standards. Engineers keep their models when they adopt ODE.
