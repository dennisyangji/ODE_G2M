Structural Moat — Architecture Gap
Dassault's CATIA/SIMULIA architecture was built in the 1990s. Binary proprietary formats are opaque to large language models. Rebuilding for AI would destroy backwards compatibility — impossible for a company protecting $5B revenue and multi-year enterprise contracts.

The moat: Modelica is equation-based and inherently machine-readable. LLMs can parse, generate, and reason about physics equations natively. This is not a feature gap — it is an architecture gap that cannot be closed by bolt-on AI features.

New Product Capabilities — Maker Platform
Maker CAD
LEGO-style modular parametric CAD — snap-together design that outputs 3D-printable files. Makes hardware design as intuitive as assembling blocks, while retaining full parametric control and AI assistance. Target: advanced LEGO hobbyists, 3D printing enthusiasts, hardware prototypers.
Firmware IDE
AI-assisted Arduino/Raspberry Pi firmware development environment — browser-based, integrated with CAD, real-time AI code suggestions, hardware pin mapping, and one-click flash. Target: Arduino developers, firmware engineers, Raspberry Pi makers, IoT builders.
