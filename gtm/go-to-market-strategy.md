# ODE Go-to-Market Strategy

## Executive Summary

ODE enters the $18B engineering simulation market as the first **AI-native full-stack engineering platform**. Our GTM strategy combines Product-Led Growth (40% of revenue) with Sales-Led Growth (60%) to reach **$1M ARR within 12 months** on a $316K marketing budget.

The core insight: engineering tools are splitting into two eras — legacy tools retrofitting AI, and AI-native platforms built for the future. ODE is firmly in the second camp, and we position ourselves as the inevitable choice for engineers who want to work with AI, not against their tools.

## Strategic Bets

### Bet 1: Modelica + AI is the Future of Engineering
**Thesis**: Modelica's equation-based, structured format is inherently more AI-friendly than proprietary binary formats. This gives ODE a structural advantage in AI-powered engineering that competitors cannot easily replicate.

**If right**: ODE becomes the default platform for AI-assisted engineering design.
**If wrong**: AI capabilities converge across all platforms, and we compete on features.
**Mitigation**: Build multi-standard support (FMI, SysML) so value isn't Modelica-dependent.

### Bet 2: Browser-First Wins Enterprise
**Thesis**: Enterprise procurement cycles for desktop software are 12-18 months. Browser-based tools can be adopted by individual engineers in minutes, creating bottom-up demand.

**If right**: PLG pipeline feeds SLG with pre-activated enterprise accounts.
**If wrong**: Enterprise IT blocks browser-based tools, requiring on-premise deployment.
**Mitigation**: Offer on-premise option for Enterprise tier.

### Bet 3: Engineers Want Unified, Not Best-of-Breed
**Thesis**: Engineers are tired of switching between CAD, CAE, and documentation tools. A unified platform with good-enough capabilities beats best-in-class point solutions.

**If right**: ODE captures full engineering workflow, high switching costs, strong retention.
**If wrong**: Engineers prefer specialized tools and resist all-in-one platforms.
**Mitigation**: Ensure each individual tool (CAD, CAE, MBSE) is competitive standalone.
