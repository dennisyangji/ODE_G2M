# ODE Content Strategy

## Executive Summary

This document defines ODE's content strategy to drive awareness, engagement, and conversion across all four ICPs (Enterprise OEMs, Engineering Consultancies, Universities, Indie Engineers). Content is the primary engine for PLG acquisition (40% of revenue) and supports SLG pipeline generation (60% of revenue) through thought leadership and demand generation.

**Goal:** Establish ODE as the category-defining AI-native engineering platform and drive toward $1M ARR through content-led growth.

---

## Content Pillars

All ODE content maps to one of four strategic pillars:

### Pillar 1: AI-Native Engineering
The thesis that engineering tools must be rebuilt from scratch around AI, not retrofitted. ODE is the first platform designed this way -- CAD, CAE, and MBSE unified in a browser with AI at the core.

- Why legacy CAD/CAE tools cannot be "AI-washed"
- The architecture of AI-native vs. AI-augmented tools
- How Claude AI transforms the engineering workflow
- Real-time AI copilot for design, simulation, and systems engineering

### Pillar 2: Modelica + AI
The convergence of open-standard modeling (Modelica) with large language models via the Modelica MCP Server. This is a unique differentiator.

- Modelica as the lingua franca of multi-domain simulation
- How LLMs understand and generate Modelica models
- MCP Server architecture and capabilities
- Practical applications: thermal, electrical, mechanical, hydraulic systems

### Pillar 3: Open Standards
ODE's commitment to open formats, interoperability, and vendor independence. This resonates strongly with enterprise buyers tired of lock-in.

- Open vs. proprietary file formats in engineering
- Interoperability as a competitive advantage
- FMI/FMU export and co-simulation
- The cost of vendor lock-in (quantified)

### Pillar 4: Engineering Productivity
Practical, measurable improvements in engineering output. This pillar is the most conversion-oriented.

- Time-to-first-simulation benchmarks
- Collaboration and version control for engineering teams
- Browser-based access: no install, no license servers
- From concept to validated design in hours, not weeks

## Social Media Strategy

### LinkedIn (Primary Channel)
**Rationale:** LinkedIn is where enterprise engineering decision-makers, consultancy principals, and university faculty spend professional time. It is the highest-ROI organic channel for B2B SaaS targeting $3,500 ARPU buyers.

**Posting Cadence:** 5x per week (weekdays)

**Content Mix:**
- Monday: Thought leadership (AI-native engineering thesis, industry trends)
- Tuesday: Product demo clip or feature highlight (30-60 sec video)
- Wednesday: Customer story or use case
- Thursday: Technical deep-dive (Modelica, simulation, AI architecture)
- Friday: Community / culture (team, events, behind-the-scenes)

**Formats:**
- Text posts with document carousels (highest organic reach)
- Native video (under 90 seconds)
- Polls (1x per month for engagement and market research)
- LinkedIn Articles (monthly, repurposed from top blog posts)
- LinkedIn Live (quarterly, tied to webinar series)

**Growth Tactics:**
- Founder and engineering team personal accounts amplify company posts
- Engage in 5-10 relevant comments daily on engineering/AI content
- LinkedIn Groups: Modelica Association, INCOSE Systems Engineering, CAE Users
- Sponsored content for top-performing organic posts ($2K/month initial budget)

### Twitter/X (Secondary Channel)
**Posting Cadence:** 3-5x per week

**Content Mix:**
- Product GIFs and short demos
- Hot takes on engineering AI news
- Thread breakdowns of technical concepts
- Conference live-tweeting
- Community engagement and retweets

**Hashtags:** #AIEngineering #Modelica #CAE #SystemsEngineering #DigitalTwin #OpenStandards

### YouTube (Long-Form Technical)
**Posting Cadence:** 2x per month

**Content Types:**
- Full product tutorials (10-20 min)
- Webinar recordings (edited with chapters)
- "Build With ODE" series: end-to-end project walkthroughs
- Conference talks and presentations
- Customer interviews

**Channel Optimization:**
- Custom thumbnails with consistent branding
- Chapters and timestamps in every video
- End screens pointing to free trial
- Playlist organization by ICP and topic

### Community Channels (Emerging)
- **Discord:** Engineering community, beta testers, power users
- **Reddit:** Participate in r/engineering, r/CAD, r/simulation (value-first, no spam)
- **Hacker News:** Submit major launches and technical blog posts
- **Modelica Forums:** Active participation, technical support, showcase ODE integrations

## Content Calendar Framework

### Monthly Planning Cycle
1. **Week 1 (Planning):** Review previous month's content performance. Identify top-performing topics, formats, and channels. Set monthly theme aligned with product/GTM priorities.
2. **Week 2-3 (Production):** Write, design, and review all content for the following month. Build a 4-week buffer for blog posts.
3. **Week 4 (Scheduling):** Schedule all social posts, queue email campaigns, finalize webinar materials.
